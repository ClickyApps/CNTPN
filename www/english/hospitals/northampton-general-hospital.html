Visiting Policies

Visiting Hours
Open visiting for parents and birthing partners. Parents are requested to nominate two other visitors per day, including grandparents.
All visitors must be accompanied by a parent unless by individual arrangement with the Ward Manager.
Visiting times 15.00-18.00 hours for all other visitors.

Numbers allowed
2 people per cot at any one time, one must be a parent.

Siblings
Can visit, but please do not bring children in who have any signs of a cough or cold or who are unwell.

Restrictions
Parents are advised not to visit if they have a cough or cold.
Grandparents/other visitors are not allowed to handle or participate in care of the baby until the baby is in the nursery.
No-one of school age is allowed to visit (except siblings).

Hygiene procedures for visitors
Facilities are available for hands to be washed at the entrance to the unit. Nursing staff advise parents/visitors of the correct technique for hand hygiene.

Neonatal unit facilities

Waiting area
Entrance area and parent’s room available.

Accommodation for parents
Room 10 can be used for parents requiring accommodation in exceptional circumstances other than prior to discharge.

Parent facilities on unit
Parents’ room with facilities for refreshments. This also contains parent information. The unit has a separate room (Room 10), which has an en-suite and is situated just outside of the unit, within the children’s ward, which is used for rooming in prior to discharge. Alternatively it can be used as a quiet place to rest.
Toilets are situated on unit (one disabled). No smoking allowed in hospital grounds.

Accommodation for siblings
None

None

Catering/refreshments
As previously stated.

Cooking facilities
Only as per parents room.

Mobile phone use
Mobile phones may be used in the parents’ room.

Telephone access
The nearest telephone is by labour ward.

Breastfeeding facilities/equipment
A breastfeeding/expressing room is available on the unit. Parents are encouraged to express at the cot side where possible.

Play area/facilities for siblings
There are a limited number of toys for young children in the parents’ room. There are no outside facilities for siblings and there are no facilities for the older sibling.

Parent support group
There is a parent support group for those who have been discharged from the Unit. The group meets on a monthly basis and is facilitated by the ward staff.

Play specialists
There are no play specialists on the unit, although the unit is well supported by play specialists from the paediatric wards are available to advise on play as appropriate.

Bereavement support
The hospital has a Bereavement suite.

Counselling service / Psychological support
No formal counselling available on the unit but staff will give support where needed.

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Ward co-ordinator or nurse looking after your baby.

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
Ward co-ordinator and/or nurse looking after your baby and Doctor on call.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Monday - starts 09.30 due to the X-ray meeting
Tuesday – Sunday starts 08.45
Parents can be updated at baby’s cot side or make an appointment to see the Doctor.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
Daily by the nurses, or as often as required. Will ring parents if a cause for concern or at parents’ request. By Doctors as necessary or at parents/nurses request.

Quiet time – when & what happens
No designated time. Rooms are kept dark when no activities taking place.
