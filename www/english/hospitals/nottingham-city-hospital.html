Visiting Policies

Parent/carers Visiting Hours
Access for parents, siblings and or official carers is open 24 hours a day.

Visiting Hours for all other family and friends
1430-2030 daily. Parents and or carers must be present at cot side with ALL other visitors.
If a single parent family negotiation can be made with staff on ward for 2nd carer for support.

Numbers allowed
2 visitors per cot and parent of carer must be present.

Siblings
Siblings can visit with their parents at all times.

Restrictions
NO children under age of 14 years unless they are a sibling.

Hygiene procedures for visitors
Gel hands on entry to unit and to each room, wash hands before patient contact. Remove coats and place in lockers available in corridor at entrance to unit. Wash hands on entry to bay. Gel available on entry to NICU and to each room. Gel hands again on leaving room and unit. Hand hygiene technique posters available at basins.

Neonatal unit facilities

Waiting area
Small area at entrance of unit.
Sitting room for visitors and families on right hand side of corridor on way to unit opposite Low dependency area.

Parent /Visitor Toilets
Toilets for parents/carers and visitors - Women on left of corridor just after entrance to Neonatal Unit and for Men on the left around the corner of corridor of main thoroughfare to unit.

Accommodation for parents
Hospital hotel: 6 Rooms are available for parents of babies being cared for in the Neonatal Unit. These rooms will be prioritised for families who come from out of Nottingham and for families of very sick Babies. However, please ask about room availability, we endeavour to cater for all families if we can. These rooms are on the top floor of the maternity building. You will be asked to sign a room agreement.
Transitional Care rooms: There are 6 en-suite rooms on the same floor as the neonatal unit and these are used for families who are nearing taking Baby home and or to establish feeding of Baby. These are part of the neonatal unit. You will be asked to sing a room agreement.

Parent facilities on unit
Parents’ sitting room (Coffee Room) with kitchen facilities with tea and coffee available. There is cereal and toast available for breakfast for parents who are staying in the hospital to be close to their baby.
There is a garden room situated close to the intensive care area for use by parents/carers only which has access to the garden courtyard.
The quiet room is also available which is situated away from the unit where families can go to if they want some quiet time and to meet with staff.

Accommodation for siblings
Cots and small beds can be available for siblings under 5 years, please ask.

Access to Unit
Parents and carers are given one access swipe card per family on admission of Baby.

Catering/refreshments
Mothers of babies in the NNU will receive meals if resident in hospital with their baby in the Hospital Hotel or in Transitional care area and or if they are at home but spending most of their time in the NNU caring for their baby. Please ask the nurse supporting you care for your baby about ordering these meals.
Breakfast is provided in the sitting room on the unit, Cereal and toast. Coffee and tea is available in kitchen situated off the sitting room.

Cooking facilities
Transitional care rooms have a kitchen area which provides a hob and microwave for cooking. This kitchen is equipped with crockery and utensils. There is also a fridge for storage of food and a designated fridge for overnight storage of breast milk.

Mobile phone use
It is important that mobile phones are switched to silent and that telephone calls are made away from the cot side, in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photographs of your baby.

Telephone access
Pay phone available in foyer of Maternity block. Unit phone can be used by families in an emergency.

Breastfeeding facilities/equipment
There is a breast expression room with 2 or more breast pumps which facilitates privacy and quiet for use by mothers at any time. The Milk Kitchen is situated next to this room to facilitate storage of breast milk. There are breast pumps available to express at the cot side if required. The Transitional Care rooms also have a breast pump to use in this area.
Breast pumps are available for loan by mothers who are at home while their baby is the neonatal unit. (These are limited in supply)

Play area/facilities for siblings
There is a toy box with toys for children to use which is situated in the parents’ sitting room (Coffee Room) where children can play and watch television. Children cannot be in this area unsupervised.

‘Little Senses’ baby group meets at Beeston Surestart Centre. Meets every Friday afternoon between 1330 and 1600hrs. Please speak to FaB (Family and baby Support) support worker on unit about details for this

FaB (Family and Baby) support
A FaB support worker comes into unit every Wednesday and will support families with the following:

  • Register families and refer families to their local Children’s Centres
  • Assist families to access other services - signposting
  • Offer practical advice on parenting and child development
  • Reduce Parental isolation
  • Offer help with bonding and attachment
  • Practical advice and guidance in caring for their baby including play and stimulation
  • Advice on speech and language development as baby progresses.
  • Helping parents to network and make friends

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Sister in Charge and the nurse who will be supporting you to care for your baby. You will be given an admission pack with information about having a baby in a neonatal unit. Nursing staff will go through this information with you.

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
Senior Doctor admitting Baby and Nurse allocated to support family in caring for Baby.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Consultant led Ward round is held from approx.1100hrs to 1300hrs and parents are encouraged to be part of this. There are Doctors hand overs at 0800hrs and 2000hrs each day. Nurse hand over at the beginning of each shift takes place at approx. 0700hrs and 1900hrs.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
The philosophy on the Unit is to have open and honest discussions with Parents at all times throughout the course of their baby’s stay. Nursing staff are proactive in their approach to sharing information. Neonatal Consultants are available every day to talk to Parents. Parents are also encouraged to telephone the Unit at any time of the day/night for an update. There is a designated direct phone number for use by families to facilitate this and this is given to families on admission of their baby and is for use by parents/ carers only. If families would like a discussion with the Consultant, at any time please ask the nurse supporting you care for your baby.

Family Centred Care
As parents you will be the main carers of your baby and we would like you to care for your baby as much as possible. The nurses supporting you care for your baby will guide you and show you how to care for your baby. The green cot-side folders are all about your baby and we would ask you to read these folders and participate in the Admission to Discharge Care Pathway. Please so not hesitate to ask about anything in this folder you do not understand. This folder can be found in the drawers of the observation chart trolley next to incubator or cot.

Quiet time – when & what happens
Quiet time is usually between the hours of 1330-1430. This is when lights are switched off and blinds are closed within the neonatal unit bays. Nursing and medical interventions with the babies are discouraged to allow the babies to have at least an hour where they can rest in a quiet darkened room. It also gives the opportunity for parents/carers time to be with their baby in calm surroundings. It is good opportunity for skin to skin contact and this is encouraged.
