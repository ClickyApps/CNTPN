Visiting Policies

Visiting Hours Parent/carers
Access for parents, siblings and or official carers is open 24 hours a day.

Visiting Hours for all other family and friends
1430-2030 daily.
Parents and or carers must be present at cot side with ALL other visitors.
If a single parent family negotiation can be made with staff on ward for 2nd carer for support.

Numbers allowed
2 visitors per cot and parent of carer must be present.

Siblings
With parents at any time.

Restrictions
NO children under age of 14years unless they are a sibling.

Hygiene procedures for visitors
Gel hands on entry to unit and to each room, wash hands before patient contact. Remove coats and place in lockers available in corridor near low dependency of unit. Wash hands on entry to bay. Gel available on entry to NICU and to each room. Gel hands again on leaving room and unit. Hand hygiene technique posters available at basins.

Neonatal unit facilities

Waiting area
At the entrance of the Neonatal Unit immediately on the right there is a small open area where families and visitors can wait.

Accommodation for parents
There are facilities for parents to stay on Ward B26. This allows families to stay close to their baby while baby is being cared for in the neonatal Unit. Location: On B Floor East Block Close to the Neonatal Unit. There are 6 rooms available. Due to security reasons on this ante and post-natal ward we cannot give access cards to parents to facilitate access to B26 rooms. Please access B26 by pressing buzzer at entrance of ward. You will be asked to sign a room agreement.
Rooming in Rooms: 2 en suite rooms are available on the Unit for use by families whose baby is very ill and/or for families about to take their baby home who may have extra needs and/or needs to establish feeding of baby. You will be asked to sign a room agreement.

Parent facilities on unit
Parent/Family Room (sitting room) is situated just a short way up the corridor after entrance to the Neonatal Unit on the left. A television is available. Families can take time out here to have short breaks if required. Small Kitchen area available where the Parent / family room is situated where families have facilities to make coffee/tea. There is a fridge in this area to store food for families if required.
The quiet room is also available which is situated away from the unit where families can go to if they want some quiet time and to meet with staff.

Costs associated with accommodation
Nil

Nearest B&Bs/hotels for other family members
Please ask staff for details if required.

Access to Unit
Via visitor’s swipe card, which are given to parents/carers on admission of baby to Unit. (one per family) These cards are for use by parents/carers only and must be returned when your baby is transferred or discharged.

Catering/refreshments
Tea / coffee making facilities in Parent / Family Room kitchen. If Mother is staying in B26 meals are provided on the ward or if staying in Rooming in Rooms, meals of packed food box is provided, this is ordered through the receptionist on the Neonatal Unit daily.
If a Mother is coming in from home and spending long periods of time caring for her Baby she can order a hot meal or snack box. Please ask Nurse supporting you care for your baby for more details.

Mobile phone use
It is important that mobile phones are switched to silent and that telephone calls are made away from the cot side, in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photographs of your baby.

Telephone access
Pay phone outside main entrance QMC and in main entrance area. Parents/carers are given direct line number to neonatal unit to allow phoning in when they are away from unit. Allowed to use internal phones in emergency.

Breastfeeding facilities/equipment
There is a breast expression room with breast pumps which facilitates privacy and quiet for use by mothers at any time. The Milk Kitchen is situated just up the main corridor to the unit on the left to facilitate storage of breast milk. There are breast pumps available in each bay to express at the cot side if required.

Play area/facilities for siblings
There is a toy box with toys for children to use which is situated in the Parents/family Room where children can play and watch television. Children cannot be in this area unsupervised.

‘Little Senses’ baby group meets at Beeston Surestart Centre. Meets every Friday afternoon between 1330 and 1600hrs.

FaB (Family and Baby)Worker
A FaB support worker comes into unit every Monday and will support families with the following:

  • Register families and refer families to their local Children’s Centres
  • Assist families to access other services - signposting
  • Offer practical advice on parenting and child development
  • Reduce Parental isolation
  • Offer help with bonding and attachment
  • Practical advice and guidance in caring for their baby including play and stimulation
  • Advice on speech and language development for when baby is progressing.
  • Helping parents to network and make friends

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Ask to see the nurse who will support you to care for your baby or the Nurse in Charge.

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
Senior Doctor admitting Baby and Nurse allocated to support family in caring for Baby. You will be given an admission pack with information about having a baby in a neonatal unit. Nursing staff will go through this information with you.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Consultant ward round takes place between 11.00 and 1300hrs.
Parents are actively encouraged to Participate in the consultant ward rounds when they take place. Doctor’s handover rounds at 8am and 8pm.
Nurse hand over at the beginning of each shift takes place at approx. 0700hrs and 1900hrs.
Please ask if you would like to speak to consultant or others in wider team on a one to one basis someone as required and this will be arranged for you.

Family Centred Care
As parents you will be the main carers of your baby and we would like you to care for your baby as much as possible. The nurses supporting you care for your baby will guide you and show you how to care for your baby. The green cot-side folders are all about your baby and we would ask you to read these folders and participate in the Discharge Care Pathway, please so not hesitate to ask about any of this folder you do not understand. This folder can be found in the drawers of the observation chart trolley next to incubator or cot.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
The philosophy on the Unit is to have open and honest discussions with Parents at all times throughout the course of their baby’s stay. Nursing staff are proactive in their approach to sharing information. Neonatal Consultants are available every day to talk to Parents. Parents are also encouraged to telephone the Unit at any time of the day/night for an update. There is a designated direct phone number for use by families to facilitate this and this is given to families on admission of their baby and is for use by parents/ carers only. If families would like a discussion with the Consultant, at any time please ask the nurse supporting you caring for your baby.

Quiet time – when & what happens
Quiet time is usually between the hours of 1330-1430. This is when lights are switched off and blinds are closed within the neonatal unit bays. Nursing and medical interventions with the babies are discouraged to allow the babies to have at least an hour where they can rest in a quiet darkened room. It also gives the opportunity for parents/carers time to be with their baby in calm surroundings. It is good opportunity for skin to skin contact and this is encouraged.
