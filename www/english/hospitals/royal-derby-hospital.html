Restrictions
No visitors are allowed without being accompanied by a parent unless pre-arranged with the parents and they have given absolute agreement and it has been recorded in Nursing Notes/Careplan.

Hygiene procedures for visitors
Hand Hygiene is the most effective way of stopping infections, we advocate hand washing as best practice and gel available on entry to NICU and parents are always taught how to wash hands on arrival and before patient contact. Hand hygiene technique cards also available for distribution.

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Entry to NICU is via a locked door, please ring the bell to gain access. Walk down the corridor and turn right to the reception desk. Ask to see the nurse caring for your baby or the Nurse in Charge.

Who will give information and updates on your baby’s condition?
The nurse caring for your baby. No information will be shared with any other person other than parents.
Staff on NICU will contact you if needed to pass on information or in an emergency, please ensure we have your contact details at all times.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Every morning starting at 9am.
Every evening between 5 and 6 pm.
Parents are actively encouraged to visit when the ward rounds are taking place, and to feel free to talk to doctors re their baby’s progress and care.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
The philosophy on the Unit is to have open and honest discussions with parents at all times, throughout the course of their baby’s stay. Nursing staff should be proactive in their approach to sharing information and planning care with parents. We promote skin-skin contact with all babies if their condition allows and actively encourage parents to learn to tube feed their babies (if required). Neonatal Consultants are available every day to talk to parents. Parents are also encouraged to telephone the Unit at any time of the day / night for an update.
We also have icoNNect a facility that allow mothers to see their baby, meet the staff providing their baby's care and discuss baby's care needs during any periods of separation through the use of iPads and Facetime. This is available for mothers who are on the labour/ postnatal ward or mothers who are unable to visit/ at home.

Quiet time – when & what happens
We aim to promote a quiet and peaceful environment at all times. The Unit is keen to promote NDCAP principles and practice and we are working hard to achieve this.
