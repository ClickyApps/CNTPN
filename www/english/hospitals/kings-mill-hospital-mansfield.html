Mobile phone use
It is important that mobile phones are switched to silent and that telephone calls are made away from the cot side, in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photographs of your baby.

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Nurse caring for your baby.

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
Nurse caring for your baby and the Doctor on the unit.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Usually late morning by on-call Consultant during week, at weekend times vary. Parents updated on ward round if available or later by Dr on unit.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
Parents usually updated daily by nurse looking after baby and by Doctor on ward round or as available.

Quiet time – when & what happens
12-2pm. Quiet time, where minimal handling advocated to promote a rest period for babies, rooms darkened and noise levels minimised but we still encourage kangaroo care and parental involvement.

Kangaroo Care
We strongly encourage and support parents to provide Kangaroo care. Please ensure you ask the nurse looking after your baby to facilitate this.
