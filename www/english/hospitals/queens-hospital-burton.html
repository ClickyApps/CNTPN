Visiting Policies

Visiting Hours
Open visiting for parents, grandparents and siblings

Numbers allowed
2 visitors per cot (A parent must be present alongside)

Siblings
All siblings allowed

Restrictions
No other children under 12 years old. Other visitors between 3-8pm accompanied by a parent

Hygiene procedures for visitors
All visitors wash their hands on entering the unit. Sink just inside the main door into the Unit Please leave your outside coats in the lockers provided inside the entrance and take your valuables with you.

Neonatal unit facilities

Waiting area
Sitting room for parents and relatives next to parents flats.

Accommodation for parents
There are two small rooms for parents to stay prior to their baby’s discharge. A chair-bed is also available in each room.. Space is quite limited. There is a shared bathroom and kitchen.

Parent facilities
2 bedrooms, sitting room, bathroom and kitchen. Television in bedrooms and sitting room. Water cooler and squash available for parents to use. Parents can make hot drinks and use the microwave in the parent’s kitchen but are asked not to bring these into the nurseries.
There are information folders for all parents in each nursery.

Accommodation for siblings
None

Costs associated with accommodation
None.

Nearest B&Bs/hotels for other family members
No known B&Bs close to the hospital, but a good selection can be found on the internet within 10-15 minutes’ drive.

Location
Off the corridor leading into the main neonatal Unit.

Access to Unit
By security phone on the main corridor.

Catering/refreshments
Cold drinks as above. Coffee, tea, milk, sugar and squash supplied by the Unit. Resident parents may bring their own food in or go to the restaurant for meals. We can provide a voucher for 1 parent towards the cost of restaurant meals. Snacks, confectionary and cereals available in the parents kitchen with donation box.

Cooking facilities
Kettle, fridge and microwave cooker in the parents kitchen

Mobile phone use
It is important that mobile phones are switched to silent and that telephone calls are made away from the cot side, in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photographs of your baby.

Telephone access
Visitors use security phone in main corridor outside the unit to gain access to the unit. No portable pay telephone. The unit’s phone can be used in an emergency.

Breastfeeding facilities/equipment
Breastfeeding room for mothers who wish to express in a quiet area. Breastfeeding is actively encouraged on the unit. Breast pumps are available for mothers to loan whilst their baby is on the unit.

Play area/facilities for siblings
Selection of toys in the sitting room for siblings to use and a couple of activity tables in the nurseries. We ask that siblings are supervised by their parents at all times.

Parent support group
Queens Early Starters meet in Burton on the first Tuesday of each month at Hill Street Health and Wellbeing Centre, Stapenhill, in Burton-on-Trent. See Information leaflet in parent’s information folder for dates and topics.

Play specialists
No designated play specialist on the Neonatal unit but there is a play specialist on children’s ward who may be available to help.

Bereavement support
There are two bereavement midwives who work within maternity and the neonatal unit.

Counselling service / Psychological support
None available on the Neonatal unit.

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Nurse in Charge

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
Nurse caring for your baby on that day

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Ward Rounds take place each morning. Parents are very welcome to attend to discuss their baby. We ask you to leave the nursery for a few minutes whilst other babies are seen. Tuesday morning rounds are teaching rounds for the doctors and medical students. You are very welcome to attend this round.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
Daily updates by the nurse caring for baby, doctors ward rounds, or by appointment.
Parents can enquire by telephone at any time, but we ask you to try to avoid staff handover times between 7.30-8am/pm.

Quiet time – when & what happens
No designated times. The unit promotes ‘quiet and quieter’. We try to dim the lights whenever possible, and to leave your baby to rest. This is in order to promote better developmental outcomes for the babies on the unit. Please feel free to ask the nurse caring for your baby for more information about this.
