What to expect on arrival

Access to the unit
Entry is via a locked door, please ring bell to gain access.

Who to ask for on arrival
The nurse in charge or the nurse caring for your baby.

Who will give initial update on baby's condition
The nurse caring for your baby can give updates at any time.
Medical staff are available during the ward rounds and upon request.

Ward Round times
Every morning between 9.30am and 11.00am

Quiet time
There is no dedicated quiet time. The unit promotes a quiet and peaceful environment at all times

Accommodation for parents
2 double bedrooms and 1 single bedroom are available on the unit.

Accommodation for siblings
Siblings may stay under exceptional circumstances by discretion of the nurse in charge.

Meal Provision
None

Self Catering Facilities
Kettle, fridge, microwave, crockery and cutlery available. Tea, coffee, milk, sugar and a selection of sweet and savoury snacks are also provided free of charge.

Mobile Phone
It is important that mobile phones are switched to silent and that telephone calls are made away from the cot side, in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photographs of your baby.

Breast Feeding Facilities/Equipment
Mobile breast pumps are available for use at the cot side.
A number of breast pumps are also available to loan for use at home whilst baby is on the unit.

Sitting Area
There is a quiet room on the unit.

Play Area/facilities
There are toys available in the kitchenette.
Children should be supervised at all times

Parent Support Group
A premature baby group is held every Thursday, 1.00pm - 3.00pm at Corby Kingswood Children's Centre, sign posting for parent support is available through this group. For further information please speak to a member of staff.
