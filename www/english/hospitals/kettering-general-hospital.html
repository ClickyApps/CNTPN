Visiting Policies

Visiting Hours
Parents 24 hours a day, other relatives 1400-1500 and 1800-1900.

Numbers allowed
3 per cot including parents.

Siblings
Siblings allowed but no other children.

Restrictions
Intensive care visiting at discretion of sister in charge.

Hygiene procedures for visitors
Wash hands, hang outside coat up and then use alcohol gel before entering unit.

Neonatal unit facilities

Waiting area
The waiting area outside the Neonatal Unit has a selection of leaflets Reviewed/updated June 2017 and a notice board. Lockers are provided with refundable £1 coin for visitors.

Accommodation for parents
The Neonatal Unit has three overnight rooms (two double and one single

Parent facilities on unit
The Neonatal Unit has three overnight rooms (two double and one single) a small kitchenette with a fridge, microwave and tea and coffee making facilities, two shower rooms and a dedicated Quiet / Counselling room.

Accommodation for siblings
Accommodation not really suitable for siblings but may stay at the discretion of the sister in charge.

Costs associated with accommodation
We do not charge for staying in the parents’ suite. However, the hospital has a flatlet, which parents can use at £35 a night, with access to free residents parking.

Nearest B&Bs/hotels for other family members
Premier Inn is 10 mins walk away and negotiated rates are available. Holiday Inn is about 10 mins away by car.

Location

Access to Unit

Cooking facilities

Mobile phone use
The Neonatal Unit advises parents to use their mobile phones purely for the purposes of taking photographs. Parents are encouraged to take telephone calls outside of the Neonatal Unit.

Breastfeeding facilities/equipment
Breast pump available to take home and Mums have their own sets on NICU to use. Freezer available to store breast milk.

Play area/facilities for siblings
The Kitchenette has a small selection of toys, books, colouring books and pencils and DVD’s as well as a television and DVD player. There is also play station for older siblings.

Parent support group
Parents are invited to a support group, which includes baby massage, following discharge; this group meets on Thursdays and is run by the local Children’s Centre.
Parents are also signposted to Bliss, Tommy’s, La Leche, National Childbirth Trust and the CNN parent Facebook page.
Bliss parent support leaflets are available on the Neonatal Unit for parents.

Play specialists
The Neonatal Unit has access to the Play Specialist team located on the Paediatric ward, Skylark.

What to expect when you arrive

Who to ask for on arrival
Ask for the Nurse in charge or the named nurse caring for their baby.

Who will give initial update on baby’s condition and timing?
The named nurse who is looking after the baby on that day.

Ward round times and whether parents are included
Daily ward rounds take place between 0930–1100 by a team of doctors and nurses led by a consultant.
Parents are encouraged to be present and will be updated with regards to their baby’s care. If parents would like to discuss their baby’s care in detail in private, the consultant is available at the end of the ward round.

Frequency of updates on baby’s condition and by whom
The nursing and medical staff are happy to update the parents regularly and as requested.

Quiet time – when & what happens
The Neonatal Unit is an advocate of family centred developmental care and parents are encouraged to be actively involved in their baby’s care from admission.
The unit does not have a specified quiet times, we do, however, endeavour to link all aspects of nursing and medical intervention together so as to minimise disruption to the baby, wherever possible.
